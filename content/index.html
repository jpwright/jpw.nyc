Jason Wright

I'm a hardware engineer interested in biosensing, healthcare, medical devices, and wireless networks. I work in the Bioelectronics and Biosensing Lab at the Feinstein Institute for Medical Research, designing low-power wireless devices for research in neuromodulation and vagus nerve stimulation in a chronic setting.

Prior to joining Feinstein, I've worked on medication adherence technology at MedicaSafe, wearable sensing devices at Intel NTG, wireless air quality sensors at Intel ICRI Cities, and DNA sequencing circuits at Intel Labs.

I'm an enthusiastic advocate for sustainable urban development, public transit, bike infrastructure, and the environment. I'm a musician, entrepreneur, and occasional artist.
